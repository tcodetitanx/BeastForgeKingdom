# BeastForge Kingdom — Lore Bible

## The Drowning

Vhal'Mora was never a gentle kingdom, but it was a living one. Its heart was the
BeastForge — a furnace older than the castle built around it — where the
Forgewardens bound the souls of beasts to men and women sworn to keep them.
A bound beast did not serve. It *belonged*, the way a heartbeat belongs.

King Maldrach the Tidebound was the greatest Forgewarden of his age, until grief
made him the worst. When the sea took his daughter, he demanded the Forge return
her. The Forge binds beasts, not the dead; it refused him. So he carried it,
ember by ember, to the cliffs — and drowned it.

The sea rose to meet the fire. It has never gone back down.

Now Vhal'Mora is a kingdom of drowned docks and lightless forests, of rain that
never quite stops and snow that falls gray. The unbound beasts went feral or
worse — hollowed, ridden by the salt-dark things that came up with the tide.
And Maldrach sits his sunken throne on the Shadow Isle, tide-bound in truth,
neither alive nor permitted to die, still asking the water to give her back.

## You

You are the last Forgewarden — an apprentice who was cleaning ash from the
Forge's grate the night the King came for it. You caught a single ember in a
lantern as the sea roared in. That ember is enough to rekindle a binding, one
beast at a time. Not enough to save a kingdom. Yet.

Four Embers of the old fire survive, held by the four Wardens-that-Were —
lieutenants of the King, changed by the drowning into something between jailer
and monument. Take the Embers back. Rebuild the Forge. Then ask the Tidebound
King, politely, to let the sea go down.

## The Wardens-that-Were

**The Rot Shepherd** (Blackroot Wilds) — once the Royal Beastmaster, Oleth
Bram. He refused to abandon the kennels when the wilds flooded; the mushrooms
kept him company, then kept him. He still shepherds — spores now, not hounds —
and he still remembers your Order's oaths well enough to hate what you're doing.

**Ghostwake, Reaver of the Deep** (The Drowned Wharf) — the King's admiral,
Seresse Vane, who scuttled her own fleet rather than let the crews flee the
kingdom. Every ship she sank sails under her now, keels-up beneath the black
water. Her Ember burns green at the mast of a ship that drowned with all hands.

**The Facet Queen** (Crystal Hollow) — the court alchemist, who tried to bottle
the Forge's fire in crystal to keep it from the King. It worked. She has been
refracting ever since — a hundred of her, one per facet, each holding a
splinter of Ember and a grudge cut precisely to your shape.

**The Tidebound King** (The Shadow Isle) — Maldrach himself, crowned in anchor-
chain. The final Ember is his daughter's lantern, which he will not look at.
He does not want to fight you. He wants you to fail, so that failing is not
only his.

## The Beasts

The Forge sorted souls by their burning: **Ember** (what consumes), **Frost**
(what preserves), **Verdant** (what grows back), **Storm** (what strikes),
**Shadow** (what remains), **Iron** (what endures), **Arcane** (what remembers).
Feral beasts can be snared and re-bound. Hollowed ones cannot — put them down
kindly. Bred beasts hatch from Forge-warmed eggs already bound, already yours;
the old Wardens called them *hearthborn* and considered them lucky.

## Maren the Forgekeeper

The breeding chambers beneath your lantern-lit camp are run by Maren — a squat,
tattooed woman who was the Forge's coal-hauler and is now, by attrition, its
high priestess. She judges your pairings, mutters over the eggs, and charges
Emberglass because "the fire doesn't feed itself, warden."

## Tone

Everything is wet, cold, and stubborn. Nobody in Vhal'Mora is cheerful and
nobody has given up, and the space between those two facts is the whole game.
